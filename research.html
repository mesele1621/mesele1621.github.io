Research

Plant Virus Discovery & Diagnostics

Working across virus–host biology, high-throughput sequencing, and molecular diagnostics to characterize emerging plant viruses and build tools for their detection and control.

Focus Areas

Core Research Themes

01

Plant Virus Discovery & Characterization

Identifying and molecularly characterizing novel plant viruses across ornamental, medicinal, and horticultural hosts, from complete genome sequencing to taxonomic classification — work behind 12 first-author "first report" and "novel virus" papers in journals such as Archives of Virology, Journal of Plant Pathology, and VirusDisease.

Genome sequencingTaxonomyNovel virus ID
02

Virus–Host Interactions & Gene Silencing

Studying how plant viruses interact with their hosts and engineering virus-induced gene silencing (VIGS) vectors to deliver genes and guide RNAs into monocot and cereal crops for functional genomics applications.

VIGSViral vectorsFunctional genomics
03

HTS-Based Virome Bioinformatics

Designing and running high-throughput sequencing and RNA-seq workflows — genome assembly, BLAST-based annotation, virome profiling, and phylogenetic analysis — to accelerate virus discovery. Applied across 18+ HTS datasets spanning 105 plant species during doctoral research.

RNA-seqGenome assemblyPhylogenetics
04

Molecular Diagnostics & Biosafety

Developing infectious clone systems and molecular diagnostic assays for virus detection and resistance screening, and conducting high-containment plant infection experiments under BSL-3 conditions in support of quarantine risk assessment.

PCR/qPCRELISABSL-3 infection assays
05

Plant Tissue Culture & Crop Biotechnology

Developing in-vitro propagation and germplasm conservation protocols for economically important crops — potato, sweet potato, ginger, bamboo, and banana — and evaluating germplasm for disease resistance under field and controlled conditions.

Meristem cultureMicropropagationGermplasm conservation
