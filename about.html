Research Expertise

Plant virus genomics, molecular diagnostics & computational virology

Mesele holds a Ph.D. in Biotechnology with a specialization in Plant Virology from the University of Science and Technology (UST) at the Korea Research Institute of Bioscience and Biotechnology (KRIBB). His research combines molecular virology, bioinformatics, and biotechnology to study emerging plant viruses and develop innovative strategies for plant disease surveillance and crop protection. His expertise encompasses virus discovery, complete viral genome characterization, high-throughput sequencing, viral metagenomics, infectious clone development, molecular diagnostics, virus–host interactions, RNA biology, plant tissue culture, and Biosafety Level 3 (BSL-3) laboratory operations.

He currently serves as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Animal and Plant Quarantine Agency (APQA) in South Korea, where he conducts research supporting national plant biosecurity and quarantine programs. His long-term research goal is to develop innovative molecular tools for virus detection, virus evolution studies, and RNA-based technologies for sustainable crop protection.
